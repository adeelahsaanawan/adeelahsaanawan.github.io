Sliding Mode Control: An Overview

Introduction

Sliding Mode Control (SMC) is a robust control strategy that deals with the uncertainty and disturbances within dynamic systems. Its principle lies in driving the system's state to a predefined sliding surface and maintaining it on this surface despite external disturbances and system parameter variations.

Mathematical Formulation

Consider a general dynamical system described by the following differential equation:

\[ \frac{dx}{dt} = f(x, u) \]\

where

  • \(x\) represents the state of the system,
  • \(u\) is the control input, and
  • \(f\) is a function of the system's dynamics.

The goal of SMC is to design a control law \(u\) that forces the system's trajectory to reach and stay on a predefined sliding surface \(S(x) = 0\), where \(S(x)\) is a continuous function of the system state \(x\).

Design of Sliding Surface

The sliding surface is designed based on the system's desired dynamics and is given by:

\[ S(x) = Cx \]\

where \(C\) is a matrix chosen such that the dynamics of the system on the sliding surface have desired properties (e.g., stability, fast response).

Control Law

The control law in sliding mode control can be defined as:

\[ u = -k \cdot \text{sign}(S(x)) \]\

where \(k\) is a positive constant, and \(\text{sign}()\) denotes the signum function, ensuring the system's trajectory reaches the sliding surface in finite time.

Conclusion

Sliding Mode Control is a powerful control technique for dealing with uncertainties and disturbances in dynamic systems. Its ability to maintain system performance despite external disturbances makes it suitable for a wide range of applications in engineering and science.
